Viking Soda Bread

Description

A variation of Irish Soda Bread, Viking Soda Bread was a staple for Noresmen and Vikings.

Bread was a staple of the diet. A person's place in the village hierarchy determined what type of bread you ate.

The slaves would eat flatbreads cooked on a griddle-type pan over a fire. Bread slightly risen would have been for the richer Vikings.

Ingredients

  • 800 g plain flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 100 g oats
  • 450 ml warm water
  • Oats to sprinkle over the top

Instructions

  1. Put the dry ingredients in a bowl and mix with a wooden spoon.
  2. Add in the water and using the spoon stir until a dough is made.
  3. Turn out onto a surface and with damp hands knead the bread until it is smooth.
  4. Place on a lined baking tray, sprinkle with the handfull of oats and into a cold oven.
  5. Set the oven to 180C (fan assisted) with a timer for 45mins and cook.
  6. Allow the bread to cool a little and then serve.
